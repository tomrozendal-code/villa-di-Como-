The Estate

A sanctuary
apart from the world

Nestled at the edge of a private lake in the Dutch countryside, Villa di Como is a study in restrained grandeur. The property traces its roots to the early twentieth century, when it served as a hunting retreat for a prosperous Amsterdam family. Today it stands fully restored — its original character preserved, its comforts entirely recast for the discerning modern traveller.

Six individually designed suites open onto terraces overlooking still water and mature parkland. A private jetty, walled kitchen garden, and grand salon — with its original oak panelling — complete the picture of a house that asks nothing of you but to slow down.

Villa di Como

Lago di Como has drawn the dreaming and the restless for centuries — its shores lined with gardens that spill into still water, its light unlike anywhere else in Europe. Stendhal called it the most beautiful place in the world. Villa di Como was built in that spirit: a place where the hush of the lake finds you, and the days lengthen into something close to grace.
